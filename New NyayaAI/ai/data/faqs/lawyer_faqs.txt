# lawyer_faqs.txt

Q: What is murder?
A: Murder is the unlawful causing of death with intention or knowledge as defined under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023.

Q: What is culpable homicide?
A: Culpable homicide means causing death by an act done with the intention or knowledge that it is likely to cause death.

Q: What is attempt to murder?
A: Attempt to murder refers to an act committed with the intention of causing death, even if death does not occur.

Q: What is causing death by negligence?
A: It means causing the death of a person through a rash or negligent act.

Q: What is hurt?
A: Hurt means causing bodily pain, disease, or infirmity to another person.

Q: What is grievous hurt?
A: Grievous hurt refers to serious bodily injuries causing severe harm.

Q: What is assault?
A: Assault is an act creating apprehension of the use of criminal force.

Q: What is criminal force?
A: Criminal force means intentionally using force against another person without consent.

Q: What is wrongful restraint?
A: Wrongful restraint means unlawfully preventing a person from moving in a direction in which they have a right to proceed.

Q: What is wrongful confinement?
A: Wrongful confinement means unlawfully restricting a person within certain limits.

Q: What is kidnapping?
A: Kidnapping means taking or removing a person unlawfully from lawful guardianship.

Q: What is abduction?
A: Abduction means compelling or inducing a person to move from one place to another by force or deceit.

Q: What is kidnapping for ransom?
A: It means kidnapping a person to demand money or other benefits for release.

Q: What is trafficking of persons?
A: Trafficking involves recruitment or transportation of persons for exploitation.

Q: What is unlawful compulsory labour?
A: It means forcing a person to work against their will.

Q: What is rape?
A: Rape is sexual intercourse without consent or under circumstances prohibited by law.

Q: What is gang rape?
A: Gang rape is rape committed by two or more persons acting together.

Q: What is sexual harassment?
A: Sexual harassment includes unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal sexual conduct.

Q: What is voyeurism?
A: Voyeurism means secretly watching or recording a person during a private act without consent.

Q: What is stalking?
A: Stalking means repeatedly following or contacting a person against their wishes.

Q: What is dowry death?
A: Dowry death refers to the death of a woman caused by dowry-related harassment.

Q: What is cruelty by husband or relatives?
A: Cruelty includes physical or mental harassment inflicted upon a woman by her husband or relatives.

Q: What is theft?
A: Theft is the dishonest taking of movable property out of the possession of another person without consent.

Q: What is snatching?
A: Snatching is the sudden taking away of property from a person's possession.

Q: What is extortion?
A: Extortion means intentionally putting a person in fear to dishonestly obtain property or valuable security.

Q: What is robbery?
A: Robbery is theft or extortion accompanied by violence or threat of violence.

Q: What is dacoity?
A: Dacoity is robbery committed by five or more persons acting together.

Q: What is criminal breach of trust?
A: Criminal breach of trust occurs when a person dishonestly misappropriates entrusted property.

Q: What is cheating?
A: Cheating means deceiving a person fraudulently or dishonestly to cause wrongful loss.

Q: What is criminal trespass?
A: Criminal trespass means entering another person's property with unlawful intent.

Q: What is forgery?
A: Forgery means creating a false document or electronic record with intent to commit fraud.

Q: What is making a false document?
A: It means dishonestly creating or altering a document to deceive others.

Q: What is using a forged document?
A: Using a forged document means knowingly using a fake document as genuine.

Q: What is unlawful assembly?
A: Unlawful assembly is a gathering of persons with a common unlawful objective.

Q: What is rioting?
A: Rioting means using force or violence by members of an unlawful assembly.

Q: What is public nuisance?
A: Public nuisance is an act causing inconvenience, danger, or injury to the public.

Q: What is giving false evidence?
A: Giving false evidence means making a false statement under oath.

Q: What is destruction of evidence?
A: Destruction of evidence means concealing or destroying material evidence related to an offence.

Q: What is criminal intimidation?
A: Criminal intimidation means threatening a person with injury to body, reputation, or property.

Q: What is defamation?
A: Defamation means making false statements that harm the reputation of another person.

Q: What is organized crime?
A: Organized crime refers to criminal activities carried out by a group for unlawful gain.

Q: What is cybercrime?
A: Cybercrime refers to unlawful activities committed using computers, digital devices, or the internet.

Q: What is hacking?
A: Hacking is unauthorized access to a computer system or network.

Q: What is identity theft?
A: Identity theft is the fraudulent use of another person's personal information.

Q: What is phishing?
A: Phishing is a cyber fraud technique used to steal sensitive information.

Q: What is online banking fraud?
A: Online banking fraud involves unauthorized or deceptive financial transactions over digital platforms.

Q: What is cyber stalking?
A: Cyber stalking means using electronic communication to harass or monitor a person repeatedly.

Q: What is email spoofing?
A: Email spoofing is sending emails with a forged sender address to deceive recipients.

Q: What is malware?
A: Malware is malicious software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access to systems.

Q: How can cybercrime be reported in India?
A: Cybercrime can be reported through the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal or at the nearest police station.
